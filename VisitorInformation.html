Practical Information about Tahiti

Location and Geography

  • Location: South Pacific Ocean, part of French Polynesia.
  • Capital: Papeete.
  • Area: Approximately 1,042 square kilometers (402 square miles).
  • Topography: Volcanic island with mountainous interior and coastal plains.

Climate

  • Type: Tropical.
  • Seasons:
    • Wet Season: November to April (humid, with heavy rainfall).
    • Dry Season: May to October (cooler and less humid).
  • Average Temperature: Ranges from 21°C (70°F) to 31°C (88°F).

Language

  • Official Languages: French and Tahitian.
  • Commonly Spoken Languages: English is widely spoken in tourist areas.

Currency

  • Currency: CFP Franc (XPF).
  • Exchange Rate: Varies, check current rates before traveling.

Getting There

  • Main Airport: Fa'a'ā International Airport (PPT) near Papeete.
  • Direct Flights: Available from Los Angeles, Auckland, Tokyo, and other major cities.

Transportation

  • Public Transport: Limited bus service around the island.
  • Taxis: Available but can be expensive.
  • Car Rentals: Popular for exploring the island at your own pace.
  • Ferries: Inter-island ferries connect Tahiti with other islands in French Polynesia.

Accommodation

  • Range: From luxury resorts and overwater bungalows to guesthouses and budget lodgings.
  • Popular Areas: Papeete, Faa'a, and the west coast for resorts; the east coast for quieter stays.

Activities

  • Water Sports: Snorkeling, scuba diving, surfing, and kayaking.
  • Nature: Hiking, waterfall tours, and botanical gardens.
  • Cultural Sites: Museums, traditional Polynesian dance shows, and historic sites.
  • Shopping: Local crafts, black pearls, and markets.

Health and Safety

  • Vaccinations: No special vaccinations required, but ensure routine vaccines are up-to-date.
  • Healthcare: Modern medical facilities available in Papeete.
  • Safety: Generally safe, but take usual precautions with personal belongings and health.

Local Customs

  • Etiquette: Greetings with a smile and "Ia Orana" (hello) are appreciated. Respect local customs and traditions.
  • Dress Code: Casual, but cover up when visiting villages or religious sites.
  • Tipping: Not customary but appreciated for exceptional service.

Useful Tips

  • Time Zone: Tahiti Time (TAHT), 10 hours behind GMT.
  • Electricity: 220-240V, European-style plugs (types C and E).
  • Internet: Available in hotels and some public areas, but can be slow.
  • Language Tip: Learning a few basic phrases in French or Tahitian can be helpful.

Emergency Contacts

  • Police: 17
  • Medical Emergency: 15
  • Fire Services: 18

National Holidays in Tahiti

National Holidays

  • New Year's Day (Jour de l'An)
    • Date: January 1
    • Description: Celebrates the first day of the Gregorian calendar year.
  • Good Friday (Vendredi Saint)
    • Date: The Friday before Easter Sunday (varies each year)
    • Description: Commemorates the crucifixion of Jesus Christ.
  • Easter Monday (Lundi de Pâques)
    • Date: The Monday following Easter Sunday (varies each year)
    • Description: Celebrates the resurrection of Jesus Christ.
  • Labour Day (Fête du Travail)
    • Date: May 1
    • Description: Celebrates workers and the labor movement.
  • Victory in Europe Day (Fête de la Victoire)
    • Date: May 8
    • Description: Marks the end of World War II in Europe.
  • Ascension Day (Jour de l'Ascension)
    • Date: 40 days after Easter (varies each year)
    • Description: Commemorates the ascension of Jesus Christ into heaven.
  • Whit Monday (Lundi de Pentecôte)
    • Date: The Monday following Pentecost (varies each year)
    • Description: Celebrates the descent of the Holy Spirit upon the apostles.
  • Bastille Day (Fête Nationale)
    • Date: July 14
    • Description: Celebrates the French National Day, commemorating the Storming of the Bastille and the unity of the French nation.
  • Assumption of Mary (Assomption)
    • Date: August 15
    • Description: Celebrates the assumption of the Virgin Mary into heaven.
  • All Saints' Day (La Toussaint)
    • Date: November 1
    • Description: A day to honor all saints, known and unknown.
  • Armistice Day (Jour d'Armistice)
    • Date: November 11
    • Description: Commemorates the armistice signed between the Allies and Germany, marking the end of World War I.
  • Christmas Day (Noël)
    • Date: December 25
    • Description: Celebrates the birth of Jesus Christ.
